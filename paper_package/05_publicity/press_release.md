## New Geometric Model of the Universe Resolves Two Major Cosmological Puzzles

*Kobrin, Belarus — April 2026* — A new study submitted to *Physical Review Letters* proposes that the Universe may have a finite, box-like shape — a "Flat Irrational Torus" — which naturally explains two of cosmology's most persistent anomalies without invoking new particles or forces.

### The Challenge

The standard cosmological model (ΛCDM) has been extraordinarily successful, yet two tensions remain unresolved:

1. **The low-ℓ anomaly:** The largest-scale patterns in the cosmic microwave background are quieter than predicted.
2. **The Hubble tension:** Measurements of the Universe's expansion rate disagree by 5 standard deviations.

Most proposed solutions add speculative new physics. This work takes a different approach: it asks whether the **global shape of space itself** could be the key.

### The Solution: A Finite, Skewed Box

The author, independent researcher Viktor Logvinovich, tested a topology where the Universe is a rectangular prism with opposite faces connected — like the video game *Asteroids*, but in three dimensions. Crucially, the box sides have irrational length ratios (√2 and √3), a configuration called the **Flat Irrational Torus (IT³)**.

This geometry has two consequences:
- It suppresses the largest-scale patterns, matching the observed "quiet" quadrupole.
- It creates a geometric link between the box size and the expansion rate, partially bridging the Hubble tension.

### Key Results

Using the latest Planck satellite data (PR4 release), the study finds:

✅ Topology scale: **Lₓ = 28.57 billion light-years** (consistent with independent geometric limits)  
✅ Hubble constant: **H₀ = 67.55 km/s/Mpc**, reducing tension with local measurements from 5σ to 2.7σ  
✅ Fit improvement: **Δχ² = −5.33** for the low-quadrupole anomaly  
✅ Validation: Model remains compatible with DESI 2024 galaxy survey and Pantheon+ supernova data

### Why It Matters

- **Parsimony:** No new particles, fields, or modified gravity — just a different boundary condition for General Relativity.
- **Testability:** The model predicts subtle signatures in future CMB polarization data (LiteBIRD) and galaxy surveys (Euclid).
- **Open science:** All code, data, and analysis scripts are publicly available for independent verification.
